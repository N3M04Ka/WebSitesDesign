Interstellar (2014)

Premiere year: 2014

Country: USA / Great Britain

Genre: Movies / Drama / Sci-Fi

Duration: 2:49

Premiere: November 5 2014

Director: Christopher Nolan

Actors: Matthew McConaughey / Anne Hathaway / Jessica Chastain / Bill Irwin / Ellen Burstyn / Michael Caine

At the end of the 21st century, food supplies on Earth are coming to an end. Crops are dying out and dust storms are occurring at an alarming rate. The government has abandoned the military, and schools are using standardized testing to determine whether a child will go to college or work as a farmer to fight food shortages. Cooper, our protagonist, is a widower and former NASA pilot whose wife died of a brain cyst that was not found because MRI machines are no longer being made (to cut down on 20th century "wastefulness"). Now he is a farmer with two children, Tom and Murphy (he is referred to as Murph throughout the film). They live with the children's maternal grandfather, Donald. In the bedroom, Murphy is visited by a ghost, whom she refers to as "ghost". Books are falling off her shelf and she believes the ghost is talking to her. Cooper tells her that ghosts don't exist and that she should use science to find out what's really going on. Shortly after, a sandstorm sweeps over their farm and enters Murph's open bedroom window, leaving Cooper to ponder what's left of him: binary code written in dust on the floor as she encounters a strange gravitational anomaly. Cooper translates the binary code and discovers a set of coordinates. He sets off in his truck to find out where they are leading, and Murph leaves with him. They arrive at an unmarked gated facility, where they are greeted by a hostile military robot named TARS, who knocks Cooper unconscious.
