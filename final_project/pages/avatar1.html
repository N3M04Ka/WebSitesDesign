In the near future, humanity is facing a global energy crisis. Man has managed to deplete Earth's natural resources, and the RDA (Resource Development Administration) sees the solution to this disaster in unobtanium, an energy-rich element that exists on Pandora's moon, a jungle planet orbiting a gas giant in the distant galaxy of Alpha Centauri. The extraction of unobtanium is a complex and expensive undertaking, not only because of the remoteness of Pandora from Earth, but also because the planet itself is dangerous to humans: Pandora's atmosphere is toxic to humans, and the Na'vi, a race of blue-colored creatures, already inhabit it and vigilantly guard it environment. Human scientists have succeeded in creating avatars - hybrid half-human-half-Navi clones that can move freely and work in Pandora's poisonous atmosphere. Human operators "upload" their consciousness into these surrogate bodies. Paraplegic Marine Jake Sully gets a second chance at life when he replaces his twin brother as an avatar operator. He is sent to Pandora to serve as a bodyguard for Dr. Grace Augustine, head of the Avatar Program, and xenobiologist Dr. Norm Spellman, who study the planet's native flora and fauna.
